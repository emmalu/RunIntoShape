Let's run and give to Charleston, all at the same time.


So, what does this mean, anyway?

For starters, Run into Shape is the "art" of planning, running and mapping a specific route so that it results in a shape when viewed from a bird's eye point of view.

More importantly:
It's a ridiculously awesome reason for runners and non-runners alike to get outside, and experience getting around our beautiful city, all while getting into shape.

Even better:
Run Into Shape is a running series and a means for donations, so that
we can help shape our community by giving to
health , environmental and academic
organizations right here in Charleston.

Bird Nerd & E-Train

 
Sarah runs to eat. Well, if you call her super slow pace “running”. Eating is one of her favorite hobbies along with birding, kayaking, dodgeball, basketball, hanging with family and friends, and pretty much anything that’s not running. BUT she’s fortunate to have fun and interesting run buddies, tolerant enough to run her pace, and help keep her motivated and in shape (while running shapes!). With a wildlife biology (UGA – Go Dawgs!) and environmental science (CofC) background, you’ll find Sarah exploring nature as much as possible, and brainstorming nature-themed shapes for upcoming runs.   Emma has a lot of ♥ for most sports and physical activities (b-ball and tennis being her most fave). And while running isn't at the top of the list, family & friends would be quick to agree that Emma loves a good challenge. So, when the idea of running courses of different shapes came about, Emma couldn't pass up the opportunity to take it to the next level. Enter: this Run into Shape movement -- a mosaic of a few of Emma's list of favorite things: rain drops on roses, playing outside, sweating and earning the joy of feasting (particularly if it involves ice cream), GIS mapping and caring for others.

Humble Beginnings

  • Our Team: Kara, Michael, Emma, Sarah
  • Race: Kiawah Half Marathon
  • Date: December 2014

Once upon a time, running may have been a laughable matter among us.
Fast forward some years and countless get-togethers, call us crazy but it appears we have experienced a radical change of heart. Now-a-days, you'll find our group to be a well-oiled machine--running forward (together and on our own) in the spirit of health and friendship.
Dispersed around town as we may be, it was this spirit and sense of adventure that led us through a detour of Park Circle--inadvertently shaping a reminiscent Pac-Man of sorts, and sparking the very beginning of this bigger and better practice of Run[ning] into Shape.
